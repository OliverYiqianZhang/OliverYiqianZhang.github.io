SOFT ROBOTIC GRIPPER

Soft robotics is a popular research direction nowadays and they have some intrinsic advantages over traditional robotics. In the project, the team designed and manufactured a pneumatic gripper along with an operating platform to perform gripping actions.

PROJECT BACKGROUND

Compared to traditional robotics, soft robotics are flexible, cheap, and easy to manufacture, rendering it a popular research direction. For instance, pneumatic grippers, which are a kind of soft robotic gripper actuated by air pressure, are especially suitable to perform relative simple actions and to handle fragile objects. The project objective is to design and manufacture a pneumatic gripper along with a bluetooth controlled robot arm system. The users should be able to control the system through remote control and collect objects on the floor.

SOFT GRIPPER DESIGN

The soft gripper is designed to be manufactured by pouring silicone into a mold. The gripper mold consists of three parts. The assembled upper and the lower mold produces the upper half of the gripper and the bottom mold produces the lower half. The two halves are then glued together. I designed a non-skid pattern on the bottom of the gripper to enable better gripping performance. 

I also designed a stepped air vent inside the gripper. As is shown in the cross sectional view, the air vents in the center are higher than the vents those in the fingers. This prevents the pressure from increasing too fast before the air reaches the finger tips.

SYSTEM DESIGN

The robot arm system adopts a four-degree-of -freedom design. At the bottom of the system, a rotary table coped with a servo allows the robot arm to rotate 360 degrees. A sliding rod attached to the top of the rotary table can tilt up and down to change the vertical location of the gripper. I designed a belt transmission system so that the power of the gear motor can be transmitted to the sliding blocks. Another servo attached to the gripper itself can tilt the gripper to grab objects from sides.

MANUFACTURING

The soft gripper is made up of the silica gel. We first mixed the type A gel and the type B gel at a ratio of 1:1 and used a vacuum pump to suck out the air bubble in the mixture. Then, we poured the mixture into the 3D printed mold and waited for 4-5 hours for the gel to solidify. The manufacturing process of the soft gripper consists of two parts. firstly, we constructed the upper part of the gripper using the upper mold and the bottom mold. After the silica gel has solidified, we took out the upper part. Then we poured a thin layer of silica gel onto the bottom mold and put the upper part onto the gel in order to seal the bottom.

FINAL RESULTS

We measured various performance metrics of our prototype. The pumping pressure inside the gripper is 64 kPa and the maximum weight that the gripper can carry is 100g. Our prototype achieved satisfactory performance on the project Gameday. The gripper succeeded in gripping a range of daily objects including packaging tape, tennis ball, plastic ball, pen etc. Our team succeeded in manufacturing the entire prototype.
