QUADCOPTER MOTOR GEARBOX

This project aims to design a planetary gearbox module for quadcopter motors that can be integrated into a bio-inspired ant robot. The gearbox should be able to effectively slow down the speed of the motor while significantly increasing the torque.

DESIGN DETAILS

I designed the carrier frame as well as the outer ring gear. I also drew the entire design in Inventor. The gearbox consists of one sun gear, three planet gears held together by the carrier frame, and one outer ring gear. To minimize production cost, the gearbox only uses small skater bearings. The entire structure is held together by the plates on all four sides with no load-bearing fasteners to avoid excessive stress concentrations when running.

TRANSFORMING MECHANISM 

To put the gearbox into application, I designed a box structure that can “sandwich” the gearbox in place using three ABS plates and one aluminum plate. The prototype gearbox functions very well in the integrated motor module. I am currently working on testing the gearbox to find out its limits. In the future, the gearbox will be used in a bio-inspired foam ant robot that I have been designing.
