SNAPLOCK

This project aims to design a universal locking mechanism to connect motor modules together for bio-inspired robots. The design allows the user to attach and detach modules with only a squeeze of the lever arms.

DESIGN BACKGROUND

Fasteners are not good enough, especially when we want to speed up the assembly process for bio-inspired snake or spider robots, which consist of a lot of motor modules. Therefore, I want a structure that can “snap-and-lock”. The idea of the project is to design a male connector and a female connector that can lock into and break away from one another through little deformation. The modularization will allow the user to extend modules freely from all four sides of the current module.

INSTALLATION PROCESS 

The design allows users to attach the male connector to the female connector by squeezing down the lever arms on the opposite sides and push forward. The socket head screws will slide into the E-shaped slot and the countersunk screws will slide into the I-shaped slot. Therefore, when locked, there will be two socket head screws constraining motion in the X-Y plane and four countersunk screws constraining motion in the Z plane. To detach, the users will just need to squeeze down and push forward again.

FINAL RESULTS

I created the CAD drawings using Inventor and the design went through several iterations. After the design was finalized, I milled the female connectors out of Aluminum plates, and laser cut male connectors out of ABS plates. The design proved to be effective and was put into application in bio-inspired robots.
