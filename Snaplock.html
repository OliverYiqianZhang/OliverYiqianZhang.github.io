SNAPLOCK

This is a mechanical design project that I worked on as a research assistant in the BIRDS Lab at the University of Michigan. ​The project aims to design a universal locking mecha​nism to connect motor modules together for bio-inspired robots. The design allows the user to attach and detach modules with only a squeeze of the lever arms.

DESIGN BACKGROUND

Fasteners are not good enough, especially when we wa​nt to speed up the assembly process for bio-inspired snake or spider robots, which consist of a lot of motor modules. Therefore, I want a structure that can “snap-and-lock”. The idea of the project is to design a male connector and a female connector that can lock into and break away from one another through little deformation. Moreover, the design pattern should be symmetrical about the diagonals so that both sides can work.

Initial Sketch

DESIGN DETAILS

After several design iterations from the initial sketech, I came up with the final design shown below. Two types of screws are used to realize the locking function. The socket head screws on the mail connector correspond to the E-shaped slots on the female connector and the countersunk screws correspond to the I-shaped slots. When combined, the relative motion between the connectors will be fully constrained. I also designed a three-sided female connector besides the single-sided version so that users can extend from the current module in four directions.

Final Design

INSTALLATION PROC​ESS 

The design allows users to attach the male connector to th​e female co​nnector by squeezing down the lever arms on the opposite sides and push forward. The socket head screws will slide into the E-shaped slot and the countersunk screws will slide into the I-shaped slot. Therefore, when locked, there will be two socket head screws constraining motion in the X-Y plane and four countersunk screws constraining motion in the Z plane. To detach, the users will just need to squeeze down and push forward again.

FINAL RESULTS

I created the CAD drawings using Inventor and the design went through several iterations. After the design was finalized, I milled the female connectors out of aluminum plates, and laser cut male connectors out of ABS plates. The design proved to be effective and is current still being developed and improved by succeeding team members.
