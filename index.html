TRANSFORMABLE ROVER

This is a virtual-prototyping project for the upper-level Design and Manufacturing course. I designed a rover with transformable wheels that can climb up and move through a sandbox as well as shrink to pass through a tunnel. The rover is also capable of distance sensing and operates automatically.

DESIGN BACKGROUND

Although wheeled mobile robots can move quickly on flat sâ€‹urfaces, they may sink into a terrain with dynamic surface such as snow, sand and small gravels. The goal of this project is to design a transformable rover to navigate all terrains. To be more specific, the rover should climb up and move through a sandbox as well as shrink to pass through a tunnel. Such transformable rovers have a wide range of applications, like in extraterrestrial exploration and in search as rescue missions.

TRANSFORMING MECHANISM 

The transformation mechanism is realized through a four-bar linkage. A servo drives the rack and pinion system and the rack is connected to the pusher. The circular frame, mated with the pusher, controls the opening and closing of the spokes through linear motion on the cylinder base. Therefore, the cylinder base, the circular frame, the connecting rods, and the spokes form a crank-slider linkage system. When the rover is climbing or moving through dynamic surfaces, the spokes will be extended to provide extra support. When the rover is back to flat surfaces, the spokes will be contracted back into the wheel.

FINAL RESULTS

Although this is a virtual-prototyping project, I validated that the design can satisfy the requirements of climbing up the sandbox and passing the tunnel through SolidWorks Motion Simulation. I also conducted force analysis and linkage animation using MATLAB. Moreover, the rover is equipped with three ultrasonic sensors, one in the front and two on the sides, so that the rover can adjust its direction and turn automatically. Beyond basic functionalities, aesthetics was also my key focus when designing the prototype.
